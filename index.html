How I Work

As a designer-in-training in SEG3125, I’m learning to build interfaces with intention, applying principles of user-centered design, visual communication, and iterative prototyping to create experiences that are meaningful and usable.

My work is grounded in empathy and ethics. I believe that technology should serve people, especially those whose voices are often left out of the design process. That’s why I take time to understand who I’m designing for—their needs, environments, and goals—before I begin sketching solutions.

My process is iterative. I start with low-fidelity sketches, evolve ideas into wireframes or mockups, and improve through feedback. I experiment and question assumptions to arrive at more inclusive outcomes.

I thrive in collaborative spaces where ideas grow through dialogue and shared insight. Even in solo work, I approach projects with the understanding that they exist within a broader ecosystem. I design with real-world impact in mind.

Whether it’s AI systems or user interfaces, I aim to design with care, creativity, and a strong sense of responsibility for the communities they affect.
